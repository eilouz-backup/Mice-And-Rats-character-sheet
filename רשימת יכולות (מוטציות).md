
**Fang & Claw**

- The Muscle — use Fang & Claw instead of The Folk when intimidating
- It's a Duel! — +3 Fang & Claw against an enemy using the same weapon
- First Bite — +2 Fang & Claw on the first turn of any confrontation
- Mouse Grip — +3 Fang & Claw to hold an enemy in place and prevent movement
- Smell of Blood — +1 to Fang & claw for each injury level you have.
- Glutton — when you defeat an enemy you can immediately attack an adjacent one

**Strings & Points**

- Take Aim — a prepared action with Strings & Points gives +3 instead of the usual taking half
- Safe Distance — +3 Strings & Points when the enemy is unaware of your presence
- Distraction Shot — attack a target to distract it, giving an ally +3 to their action that are not attacks
- Improvised Throw — +3 when using environmental objects as improvised thrown weapons
- Second Chance — once per encounter, a Strings & Points failure doesn't waste your turn
- Two Targets — once per encounter, hit two adjacent enemies with a single roll

**Ha! Missed me!**

- Poker Face — can lie using Ha! Missed me! instead of The Folk
- By a Whisker — +3 Ha! Missed me! in tight or confined spaces
- Small and Fast — +3 Ha! Missed me! against significantly larger enemies
- Combat Dance — when you dodge by 5 or more, you can immediately roll Fang & Claw
- Like Water — once per session, turn a hit that would cause injury into a disadvantage with no damage
- Leg Pull — once per session a Ha! Missed me! success lets you redirect an enemy's attack into their ally



**The Rat**

- Something Feels Off… — +3 to The Rat or The Folk to detect a lie
- I Can Smell You're Lying — +3 to The Rat or The Folk to draw information from an enemy
- Kung Fu! — spend adaptation to force an enemy to defend with The Rat instead of Ha! Missed me!
- Sixth Sense — once per encounter, ask the GM if something in the scene is dangerous
- Prey on the weak — +3 The Rat at the start of a confrontation to identify the weakest enemy
- Alert — the character is never fully surprised; always gets a basic reaction


**The Folk**

- Full Steam Ahead — +3 The Folk when making a first impression
- They Probably Remember Me — +3 The Folk when searching for contacts or allies
- Full of Pride — if the roll immediately after a blow-up is The Folk, add +5
- Knows the Game — +3 The Folk against authority figures
- Agitator — roll The Folk to make an enemy attack you instead of a friend
-  I Can Smell You're Lying - +3 to The Rat or The Folk to detect a lie
- Something Feels Off… - +3 to The Rat or The Folk to draw information from an enemy
- New Face — +3 The Folk when impersonating a completely different person
- Disarming Humor — +3 The Folk to defuse tension in a high-pressure scene
- Perfect Victim — +3 The Folk when playing the role of someone weak or innocent
- Dangerous Charisma — +5 The Folk after a Fang & Claw blow-up in the same encounter

**Crafty**

- Escape Artist — +3 Crafty to escape restraints and pick locks
- Just Nudge It a Little — +3 Crafty to make equipment function differently than intended
- Use What's There — any environmental object can serve as a tool with +2
- Mechanical Analysis — +3 Crafty to understand how something works without dismantling it
- Miniaturize — take large equipment and make it portable with a Crafty check
- Combat Engineer — roll Crafty instead of Fang & Claw to use the environment against an enemy

**Light Feet**

- One with the Shadows — +3 Light Feet when sneaking
- Flight of the Crane — +3 Light Feet when at height or jumping across a gap
- - Mouse Roll — +3 to light feet when avoiding fall damage 
- Like Wind — +3 Light Feet when moving through a dense crowd
- Crack in the Wall — +3 Light Feet to pass through small openings or gaps



**General**

- Experienced — choose a skill, all rolls in it get +1 _(can be taken once per skill)_
- Skilled — _(replaces Experienced)_ +3
- Expert — _(replaces Skilled)_ +5
- Quick Healing — can recover one injury level at the end of a scene
- Gritted Teeth — to reach the third injury level the character must fail by 10 or more
- One for All — can spend adaptation tokens for friends at 1:1 rate
- Lucky _(once per encounter)_ — spend 2 chaos to reroll
- Nerves of Steel — spend adaptation to treat a snap decision as a prepared action
- Count on Me! — when using a prepared action, the die counts as one size larger
- Flashback — create a small scene where you did something helpful earlier; afterward you can assist another player even if your character isn't present
- Loyal Companion — once per encounter, prevent an ally from taking an injury by taking it yourself
